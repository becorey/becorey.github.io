Design and Engineering

Designing products for the modern world

de·sign: the process by which concepts are materialized into reality

The world today is a living ecosystem of products. Every day, firms release new designs, factories build finished goods, and people go shopping; it's an amazing buzz of activity.

The process is evolutionary. Good designs are bought up, prompting more copies to be produced, while bad designs die in the clearance aisle. Designers are the active agents who constantly listen to their users, observe industry trends, and build innovation into real, physical things.

Portfolio

My Work

Sensi™ Touch Wi-Fi Thermostat by Emerson
Released June 2017
I was lead mechanical engineer and industrial designer for the new product development of this retail electronic thermostat.
The project involved industrial design sketching and modeling, quick prototyping, competitor analysis, new plastic part design, PCB component tracking for mechanical integration, thermal management, and packaging design. The project was successful and this product is currently sold through Amazon, Lowes, and Home Depot.

  • Collaborated between marketing, electrical, and mechanical teams to meet all design requirements.
  • Worked with overseas team and suppliers for design development and part procurement.
  • Worked with contract manufacturer to scale up to full production.

DGC-2020HD by Basler Electric
Released August 2015
This project was an upgrade to the existing product. It consisted of upgrading the small monochrome LCD to a 4.3" color LCD, and upgrading the graphic overlay to a membrane switch with an integrated resistive touch screen. The project involved assessment of the marketing requirements and determining design direction​, new plastic part design, modification to an existing plastic part, new sheet metal design with hardware inserts, new gasket design, new membrane switch design, and working with electrical engineers for PCB modification. Testing involved HALT, vibration, extreme temperature, and IP sealing. The project was successful and the product is currently available.

Biomass Recycling Facility by Chip Energy
This project involved the co-design and documentation of Paul Wever's concept for a biomass recycling facility. It involved 3D CAD modeling of the entire facility and the equipment within; bulk material conveyors, storage silos, and structural elements such as the roof and foundation.
